• Used for lists with simple details, such as a glossary
  • bookmarkAdaptation
    The adjustment in natural or human systems in response to actual or expected climatic stimuli or their effects, which moderates harm or exploits beneficial opportunities.
  • bookmarkCapitals
    The Five Capitals are Natural Capital (Natural resources and ecosystem services) Physical Capital (infrastructure and the built environment), Financial Capital (level, variability and diversity of income sources, access to wealth, and access to finance), Human Capital (skills, health, education, information), and Social Capital (social networks, governance, formal/informal institutions, etc). https://www.forumforthefuture.org/project/five-capitals/overview
  • bookmarkCoping capacity
    The ability of people, organizations and systems, using available skills and resources, to manage adverse conditions, risk or disasters. The capacity to cope requires continuing awareness, resources and good management, both in normal times as well as during disasters or adverse conditions. Coping capacities contribute to the reduction of disaster risks.
  • bookmarkDisaster
    A serious disruption of the functioning of a community or a society at any scale due to hazardous events interacting with conditions of exposure, vulnerability and capacity, leading to one or more of the following: human, material, economic and environmental losses and impacts.
  • bookmarkDriver
    The drivers, and resultant pressures, are the causes of change (direct or indirect) which have an impact on the Stock/Capital under investigation.
  • bookmarkEcosystem Services
    The benefits that people and communities obtain from ecosystems.
  • bookmarkExposure
    People, property, systems, or other elements present in hazard zones that are thereby subject to potential losses.
  • bookmarkHazard
    A dangerous phenomenon, substance, human activity or condition that may cause loss of life, injury or other health impacts, property damage, loss of livelihoods and services, social and economic disruption, or environmental damage.
  • bookmarkImpact
    The likely effect of change. FOrmally definied as the product of Exposure and Susceptibility.
  • bookmarkMitigation
    The lessening or minimizing of the adverse impacts of a hazardous event.
  • bookmarkOpportunity
    The benefits that may be identified through the process of a risk assessment. Global change will produce some positive impacts that may provide opportunities to improve economic, environmental and social outcomes (Queensland Government, 2011).  
  • bookmarkPressure
    Results from variation in drivers away from their typical baseline.
  • bookmarkResilience
    The ability of a system, community or society exposed to hazards to resist, absorb, accommodate to and recover from the effects of a hazard in a timely and efficient manner, including through the preservation and restoration of its essential basic structures and functions.
  • bookmarkRisk
    The UNISDR defines risk as the combination of the probability of an event and its negative consequences.  Probability is derived from frequency of events and/ or the certainty associated with the event. We have included in this definition the expected lead time to mitigation of the event, and therefore the urgency in relation to an impending risk.  
  • bookmarkStock
    A stock refers to a system of interest, the state of which is impacted by hazards.
  • bookmarkSusceptibility
    The aspect of a stock sensitive to a hazard.  
  • bookmarkVulnerability
    The characteristics and circumstances of a community, system or asset that make it susceptible to the damaging effects of a hazard.  Vulnerability of a system would refer to the: Exposure to a hazard (ie the degree, duration, and/or extent in which the system is in contact with, or subject to, the environmental, economic, or socio-political stress); Sensitivity to that hazard (the degree to which a system is modified or affected by a disturbance); and the Capacity of the system to cope, adapt, or recover from the effects of those conditions.
